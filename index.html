I am currently in the process of obtaining my degree at the University of California, Riverside. As of now, I have completed one full college year here at this university; thus making me a second-year CHASS student majoring in Political Science. I made the decision to major in Political Science because, in a couple of years, I plan to be a criminal defense attorney. Not only that, but I feel as though it is best to obtain some knowledge of politics, especially in our current political climate that we live in. To continue from my previous statement of what I strive to accomplish as my future career, I want to be a criminal defense attorney for many reasons. One important reason as to why I would like to be a criminal defense attorney is the fact that I believe that there are many injustices that occur in the legal system. It is no secret that racist ideologies have become justifications for the exploitation of people of color, which is what shaped our laws and decision-makers in our criminal justice system for centuries. Once I become a criminal defense attorney, I would have some type of power in aiding the people to a more idealistic pathway to a better lifestyle compared to the continuous cycle of throwing those in prison to be caged up, instead of providing rehabilitation. Too many people of color are getting thrown into cells for a longer sentence than those who have done much worse due to the color of their skin. I want to make a difference and become the change we have all wanted to see in the twisted system we currently have. Instead of seeing these defendants as criminals, I prefer to see them as good people who made mistakes. With this mindset implemented, more people would encourage rehabilitation over prison terms.
